The Natural Simulations module in Khan Academy demonstrates how computer science is linked to math concepts and how those concepts can be applied to simulate a natural environment. An aspect of the module that impacted me the most was how I could use different types of randomness to mimic real-world scenarios, such as the project below, which explores a simulation of Algae’s interaction with bacteria. In this project, I incorporated randomness to simulate how the ecosystem occurs to that in real life. Moving forward, I will continue to use these math concepts to further enhance my understanding of computer science, as well as apply that knowledge in applicable scenarios.
